Things to consider when ordering your broadband.

Once you know the type of broadband you can get at your place there are some things to think about and ask your broadband provider to help make your home broadband the best it can be.

Tips for the ultimate WiFi set up.

With Wireless, ADSL and VDSL the speed you can get at your home or business is dictated by how far away you are from the nearby cabinet. However with Fibre speed you order the speed you need. Speeds range from 50mbps up to 1000mbps (The Gig).

Tip: For the best experience we recommend selecting 100mbps or above to not limit the power of your fibre connection.

Ask your broadband provider

  • Is this the fastest/best broadband connection I can get at my house?
  • What speed am I going to get at my place?
  • What may affect the speed I get at my house?

How much data?

With data it’s important to not just think about your current usage but what you might use in the future. In the next 12 months, could you be video calling family overseas or watching TV series or movies online?

Tip: Use our data calculator to help you estimate how much data you may need. If in doubt we recommend going on an unlimited plan so you never have to limit your usage.

Ask your broadband provider

  • If I buy a capped plan today can I increase it free of charge within my contract period if my usage changes?

Getting the right modem and setup?

Making sure you have a good modem that sets you up for the future will make things easier as you upgrade over time. Making sure you’re getting a fibre ready modem and checking it will be enough to provide Wi-Fi coverage across your home will help give you a better experience.

Ask your broadband provider

  • Is my modem fibre ready?
  • Is one modem going to be sufficient to stretch the Wi-Fi across my house?
  • Will I need more than one modem? Will you support it/provide support for a second modem?
